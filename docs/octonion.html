Octonion Numbers

This is an example of a dependent field. The Octonion numbers can be over any field such as the Natural numbers, the integers, or the rationals or other Octonion numbers. Octonions are the field extension \(\mathbb{F}[I,J,K:I^2=J^2=K^2=IJK=-1]\). Where \(IJ=-JI\) To create a Octonion number we can write: We can multply two Octonion numbers together: $$\sqrt{1}$$
